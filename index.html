ABOUT ME

...

I started potting in 1999 at Crouch End Art School with Romilly Graham. I make functional art ceramics and enjoy experimenting with many techniques.

A branch of my hobby overload is also being an addicted knitter. The yarn bowls I saw at fairs lacked a knitters touch and understanding. I made five bowls for friends and family, this led to requests from their friends and suggestions that I should sell them. This led to starting The London Yarn Bowl (LYB) in 2014. It has been a friendly venture with ceramic friends Lucky Rutland, Toby Dacre, Mark Brooker and Millie-Beth Wright all contributing their creativity.

The LYB hopes to share creativity and a love of making useful, well made objects with discerning knitters and crocheters - people like us who get pleasure from craft. â€‹Every LYB is handmade and hand decorated using stoneware, it is twice fired in an electric kiln. Now in its fourth year the LYB has made about 200 bowls. Every bowl is truly unique. I am happy to take on commissions for yarn bowls and other projects, please get in touch to organise this. No project is too small or too big. My smallest commission was a cat mug, my largest was the tableware for a North London restaurant.

My next exhibition is 10 November 2017, please get in touch for an invite.

Happy Knitting, Jayne Wright
